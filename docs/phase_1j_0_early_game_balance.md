# Phase 1J.0 Early-Game Balance Note

## Intended early loop

- The first safe money comes from short legal trade hops around Fort Bush, Newark Station, and Rochester Base.
- Legal runs should feel reliable and modest, with small but consistent margins rather than huge arbitrage.
- Bounty and escort jobs should outpay simple hauling, but they should still feel like "work" rather than instant wealth.
- Contraband runs through Buffalo Base should be the strongest early money maker, but only if the player accepts scan risk and possible cargo loss.
- Cargo pod loot should be worth picking up, but not so valuable that a single fight resets progression.

## Intended route shape

- Legal route: Fort Bush -> Newark Station for food, water, and fuel.
- Secondary legal route: Newark Station -> Rochester Base for industrial goods and general trade.
- Contraband route: Buffalo Base -> Rochester Base for side arms and alien cargo.

## Expected first upgrades

- A player should be able to afford a small upgrade after a few missions or a few good trade legs.
- The first purchases that should feel realistic are light utility upgrades, then better weapons or ship systems after a few more jobs.
- Big-ticket changes, like buying into a new ship or stacking multiple systems at once, should take noticeably longer.

## Balance knobs to revisit later

- Mission reward ranges by difficulty.
- Delivery distance bonus and bounty target bonus.
- Station market buy/sell spreads on Newark, Rochester, Buffalo, and Detroit.
- Contraband scan pressure and police response timing.
- Cargo pod pool weighting and quantity ranges.
- Starter equipment prices and ship dealer trade-in pacing.
